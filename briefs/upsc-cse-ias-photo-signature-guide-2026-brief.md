## Key Unique Angles (not covered by any competitor)

1. **Live webcam matching (2026 new)** — UPSC CSE 2026 introduced a live webcam step during form submission. Portal captures face live and matches against uploaded photo. No competitor explains the mechanics or the practical implication (must look like you right now, not a year-old photo).

2. **Triple signature requirement** — three cursive signatures written vertically on a single sheet. All competitors mention "sign three times" without explaining: one sheet only, vertical arrangement, consistent style, one image upload. Confusion point: candidates try to upload three separate files.

3. **10-day recency strictness** — CSE and CDS share the 10-day rule (NDA allows 6 months). An NDA-compliant photo from 3 months ago fails the CSE portal check. This cross-exam trap is unaddressed in any SERP result.

4. **200 KB vs 300 KB discrepancy** — honest disclosure that coaching sources citing 200 KB are referencing pre-OTR notifications; current OTR standard is 300 KB. Being the only source that explains this discrepancy (rather than picking one value) is a competitive trust signal.
