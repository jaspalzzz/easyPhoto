### FAQ Section (5 items)
1. **Can I wear spectacles in my SSC CGL photo?**
   No. SSC bans glasses in all exam photos. Remove them for the live capture — even prescribed medical glasses are not permitted.

2. **Can I upload an old photo from my gallery for SSC CGL?**
   No. The current SSC portal requires live photo capture via webcam or phone camera. The "upload from file" option no longer exists. If you are on a desktop, ensure your browser has webcam permission.

3. **What is the maximum file size for an SSC CGL photo?**
   50 KB. But the portal also enforces a minimum of 20 KB — a photo compressed below 20 KB is rejected just as firmly as one above 50 KB. Use an exam-specific resizer that targets the full 20–50 KB band.

4. **My SSC signature looks fine but the portal rejects it. Why?**
   Three common causes: the signature is written in ALL CAPITALS (portal checks for this), the file is outside the 10–20 KB band, or the paper background shows grey instead of white (use the signature resizer to clean the background).

5. **Is the SSC CGL photo spec the same as IBPS PO?**
   Almost identical: both want 20–50 KB JPG with a white background. IBPS PO confirms 3.5 × 4.5 cm (200 × 230 px). SSC CGL uses the same physical size. Use the exam-specific resizer for each to ensure the exact portal dimensions match.
