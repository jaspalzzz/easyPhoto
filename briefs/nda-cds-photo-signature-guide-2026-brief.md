### H2: Why is the NDA/CDS photo square — and why your banking exam photo won't work? (250–300 words)
- **Answer-first:** "The UPSC OTR portal requires a minimum square crop (350×350 px). Most banking exam portals like IBPS use a portrait rectangle (200×230 px). These are not interchangeable — a 200×230 portrait image uploaded to the UPSC portal fails the pixel check."
- **Cover:**
  - Why this catches cross-applicants: a candidate applying to both IBPS PO and NDA 2 in the same window uses the IBPS portrait crop for NDA and hits an error
  - What square means in practice: face centred, equal space on left/right, more space for shoulder/chin than a portrait crop
  - The `/upsc-photo-resizer/` outputs the correct square crop automatically

- **Answer-first:** "Plain white background, formal attire, face covering at least 75% of the frame, no spectacles."

### FAQ Section (5 items)

1. **Is the NDA photo size the same as the CDS photo size?**
   Yes. Both NDA and CDS use the same UPSC OTR portal with identical specifications: 20–300 KB, JPG, minimum 350×350 px, plain white background, name and date printed at the bottom. There is no difference between the two exams' photo or signature requirements.

2. **Does the NDA application photo need my name and date on it?**
   Yes, and this is mandatory — not optional. Your full name (as on the notification) and the date the photograph was taken must appear as printed text at the bottom of the image. Add it using the photo-with-name-date tool before uploading.

3. **Can I wear glasses in my NDA or CDS photograph?**
   No. UPSC banned spectacles in NDA and CDS application photos. Even prescription glasses are not permitted — remove them for the photograph. The only exception is a medical certificate specifically mentioned in the notification.

4. **Why won't my IBPS PO photo work for NDA?**
   IBPS PO requires a portrait-rectangular photo (200×230 px). NDA requires a square-minimum photo (350×350 px). Uploading the IBPS crop to the UPSC portal fails the pixel check. Use the UPSC photo resizer, which applies the correct square crop for defence exam applications.

5. **My NDA photo is under 20 KB — will it be accepted?**
   No. The UPSC portal enforces a minimum of 20 KB as well as the 300 KB ceiling. A photo compressed below 20 KB is rejected for being too low-quality to read. Use the UPSC photo resizer, which targets a file size inside the 20–300 KB band.
